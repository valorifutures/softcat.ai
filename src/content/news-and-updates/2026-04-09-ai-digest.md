This week brought proper infrastructure for AI agents and some genuinely impressive open-weight models. The research automation tools are getting scary good.

## OSGym makes agent training actually affordable

Training computer-use agents just got 100x cheaper. [OSGym](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/04/08/meet-osgym-a-new-os-infrastructure-framework-that-manages-1000-replicas-at-0-23-day-for-computer-use-agent-research/) manages over 1,000 OS replicas for just $0.23 per day, solving the massive infrastructure headache that's been holding back agent research. This is the kind of boring but crucial work that actually moves the field forward.

## Google's PaperOrchestra writes research papers end-to-end

Google's [PaperOrchestra](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/04/08/google-ai-research-introduces-paperorchestra-a-multi-agent-framework-for-automated-ai-research-paper-writing/) uses multiple AI agents to turn messy lab notes and experimental results into polished academic papers. The multi-agent approach handles everything from structuring arguments to formatting citations. We're genuinely curious how long before this becomes standard practice in academic labs.

## Z.AI drops massive open-weight coding model

[GLM-5.1](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/04/08/z-ai-introduces-glm-5-1-an-open-weight-754b-agentic-model-that-achieves-sota-on-swe-bench-pro-and-sustains-8-hour-autonomous-execution/) is a 754B parameter model built specifically for agentic tasks, not just chat completions. It hits state-of-the-art on SWE-Bench Pro and can run autonomously for 8 hours straight. The fact it's open-weight makes this particularly significant for anyone building serious coding agents.

## Astropad builds remote desktop for AI agents

[Astropad's Workbench](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/08/astropads-workbench-reimagines-remote-desktop-for-ai-agents-not-it-support/) lets you monitor and control AI agents running on Mac Minis from your iPhone. Low-latency streaming designed for watching agents work, not fixing your mum's printer. Smart pivot from their traditional creative tools business into the agent monitoring space.
