Everyone's building agents but nobody knows how to control them properly.

## Mistral launches remote coding agents with 77.6% SWE-Bench score

[Mistral AI](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/05/02/mistral-ai-launches-remote-agents-in-vibe-and-mistral-medium-3-5-with-77-6-swe-bench-verified-score/) dropped async cloud-based coding sessions alongside their new 128B flagship model and agentic Work mode in Le Chat. The 77.6% SWE-Bench verified score puts them firmly in competitive territory for actual development work. This feels like the moment coding agents move from demos to proper tooling.

## OpenAI turns on ad tracking by default for ChatGPT users

[OpenAI quietly enabled](https://the-decoder.com/chatgpt-now-tracks-users-for-ads-by-default-as-openai-looks-for-new-revenue/) marketing cookies by default for free ChatGPT users in countries running ads. Paying subscribers stay untracked, which is a clever way to push premium subscriptions. The revenue hunt is clearly accelerating as the honeymoon period of free AI ends.

## AI governance lags behind agent deployment

[Australian regulators flagged](https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/ai-agent-governance-control-gaps/) massive control gaps as financial firms rush to deploy AI agents without proper governance frameworks. Meanwhile, [ARC-AGI-3 analysis](https://the-decoder.com/even-the-latest-ai-models-make-three-systematic-reasoning-errors-arc-agi-3-analysis-shows/) shows even GPT-5.5 and Opus 4.7 still make systematic reasoning errors that humans avoid easily. We're deploying systems faster than we understand their failure modes.

## Meta acquires robotics startup for humanoid push

[Meta bought Assured Robot Intelligence](https://the-decoder.com/meta-acquires-assured-robot-intelligence-to-accelerate-humanoid-robot-push/) to accelerate their humanoid robot work, aiming for an Android-style open platform. Smart move, but the real test will be whether they can avoid the hardware graveyard that claimed so many previous Meta projects.
