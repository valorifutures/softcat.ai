The Pentagon-AI lab drama is heating up, with real consequences for who gets to build military AI systems.

## OpenAI signs Pentagon deal after Anthropic refuses

OpenAI just inked a defense contract with what Sam Altman calls ["technical safeguards"](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/28/openais-sam-altman-announces-pentagon-deal-with-technical-safeguards/) while [the Pentagon moves to blacklist Anthropic](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/27/pentagon-moves-to-designate-anthropic-as-a-supply-chain-risk/) as a supply chain risk. This comes after Anthropic refused to allow "all lawful use" of Claude for military purposes. The real story here is how corporate AI ethics stances are now directly affecting national security contracts and competitive positioning.

## Alibaba open-sources CoPaw agent workstation

Alibaba's team released [CoPaw](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/03/01/alibaba-team-open-sources-copaw-a-high-performance-personal-agent-workstation-for-developers-to-scale-multi-channel-ai-workflows-and-memory/), an open-source framework for scaling multi-channel AI agent workflows. The timing feels deliberate as more developers need proper tooling to move beyond simple LLM calls to actual autonomous systems. Worth watching if you're building anything with persistent agent memory.

## ChatGPT hits 900M weekly users

OpenAI announced [ChatGPT reached 900 million weekly active users](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/27/chatgpt-reaches-900m-weekly-active-users/) alongside their massive $110 billion funding round. That's genuinely staggering adoption for an AI tool that didn't exist three years ago. The user growth shows we're past the novelty phase and into real utility territory.

## Sakana AI's instant model adaptation

Sakana AI dropped [Doc-to-LoRA and Text-to-LoRA](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/02/27/sakana-ai-introduces-doc-to-lora-and-text-to-lora-hypernetworks-that-instantly-internalize-long-contexts-and-adapt-llms-via-zero-shot-natural-language/), hypernetworks that can instantly adapt LLMs using natural language prompts. This could solve the painful trade-off between in-context learning flexibility and fine-tuning efficiency. Early days but the approach looks promising for rapid model customization.
