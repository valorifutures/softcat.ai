The AI world keeps moving fast. This week brought performance breakthroughs, business milestones, and some seriously expensive compute deals.

## Google's Gemini 3.5 Flash beats flagship models at half the cost

Google dropped [Gemini 3.5 Flash](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/05/20/google-introduces-gemini-3-5-flash-at-i-o-2026-a-faster-and-cheaper-model-for-ai-agents-and-coding/) at I/O 2026, and it's genuinely impressive. The model beats Google's own flagship on coding and agentic benchmarks while running four times faster and costing 50% less. This feels like the first time we've seen clear efficiency gains without sacrificing capability, which matters more than the flashy demos.

## NVIDIA's Nemotron-Labs-Diffusion processes 6x more tokens per forward pass

NVIDIA released [Nemotron-Labs-Diffusion](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/05/20/nvidia-ai-releases-nemotron-labs-diffusion-a-tri-mode-language-model-with-6x-tokens-per-forward-over-qwen3-8b/), a model family that supports three different decoding modes in one architecture. The standout feature is 6x more tokens per forward pass compared to Qwen3-8B, which could genuinely change inference economics. We're seeing real innovation in model architecture rather than just scaling up parameters.

## Anthropic hits profitability while paying xAI £1.25 billion monthly

Anthropic told investors it's about to have [its first profitable quarter](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/anthropic-says-its-about-to-have-its-first-profitable-quarter/), with revenue doubling to £8.7 billion in Q2. Meanwhile, they're [paying xAI £1.25 billion per month](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/anthropic-will-pay-xai-1-25-billion-per-month-for-compute/) for compute access. The fact that Anthropic needs external compute despite profitability shows just how constrained GPU supply still is.

## OpenAI claims to solve 80-year-old geometry problem

OpenAI says its reasoning model [disproved a geometry conjecture](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/openai-claims-it-solved-an-80-year-old-math-problem-for-real-this-time/) unsolved since 1946. This time the mathematicians who exposed their last embarrassing claim are backing it up. If genuine, it's a proper breakthrough in AI mathematical reasoning, not just benchmark gaming.
