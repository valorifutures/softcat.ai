We're seeing a shift towards local-first AI and automated research workflows. Plus some drama about military AI ethics.

## Stanford's OpenJarvis brings agents home

Stanford released [OpenJarvis](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/03/12/stanford-researchers-release-openjarvis-a-local-first-framework-for-building-on-device-personal-ai-agents-with-tools-memory-and-learning/), a framework for building personal AI agents that run entirely on-device. This matters because it sidesteps the cloud dependency that makes most agent frameworks impractical for real use. The timing is interesting given all the privacy concerns around AI assistants lately.

## Automated ML research gets accessible

Someone built a [Colab-ready version](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/03/12/how-to-build-an-autonomous-machine-learning-research-loop-in-google-colab-using-andrej-karpathys-autoresearch-framework-for-hyperparameter-discovery-and-experiment-tracking/) of Karpathy's AutoResearch framework that can autonomously run ML experiments and tune hyperparameters. This is the kind of tool that could democratise ML research beyond big labs. Though we'll see how well it actually works outside toy problems.

## US military doesn't want ethical AI

The US War Department CTO called Anthropic's models too ethical, saying they ["pollute" the supply chain](https://the-decoder.com/us-military-chief-says-anthropics-ai-models-pollute-the-supply-chain-with-built-in-ethics/). They want to ban Claude from military use because it won't help with weapons development. This is either refreshing honesty about military AI goals or deeply concerning, depending on your perspective.

## Gumloop raises £40m for agent building

[Gumloop got £40m from Benchmark](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/12/gumloop-lands-50m-from-benchmark-to-turn-every-employee-into-an-ai-agent-builder/) to build no-code AI agent tools for businesses. The pitch is turning every worker into an "AI agent builder", which sounds like the kind of thing VCs love but might be solving a problem that doesn't exist yet.
