Symbolic regression is being pitched as the solution to AI's black box problem. But this isn't really about making models more interpretable. It's about turning neural networks into something every Excel jockey can understand and modify.

## The great equation flattening

Converting a trained model into a mathematical equation fundamentally changes what you're working with. Your carefully tuned transformer becomes a polynomial. Your vision model becomes a series of algebraic expressions. The complexity that made these models powerful gets compressed into something a first-year maths student could manipulate.

This isn't preservation, it's translation. And like all translations, something gets lost. The emergent behaviours, the subtle interactions between layers, the implicit knowledge encoded in weight relationships. All flattened into explicit mathematical operations.

## Commoditisation by simplification

The real game here isn't explainability. It's making AI accessible to people who don't want to deal with GPUs, model weights, or inference servers. Once your model is an equation, it runs anywhere. No frameworks, no dependencies, just basic arithmetic operations.

We're witnessing the Excel-ification of machine learning. Soon, your carefully crafted neural architecture will be reduced to a formula that fits in a spreadsheet cell. The mystique of AI disappears when anyone can inspect the maths and tweak the coefficients.

That might be exactly what the industry needs.
